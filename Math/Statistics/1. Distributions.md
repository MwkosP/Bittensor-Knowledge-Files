



# Statistics — Distributions

---

## What a Distribution Is

A distribution describes how probability is spread over possible values of a random variable.

|Term|Meaning|
|---|---|
|Random variable $X$|A variable whose value is uncertain|
|PMF $P(X=x)$|Probability mass function — discrete|
|PDF $f(x)$|Probability density function — continuous|
|CDF $F(x)$|Cumulative: $P(X \leq x)$|

---

## Key Statistics of a Distribution

| Statistic | Formula                                   | Meaning            |
| --------- | ----------------------------------------- | ------------------ |
| Mean      | $\mu = \mathbb{E}[X] = \sum x \cdot P(x)$ | Expected value     |
| Variance  | $\sigma^2 = \mathbb{E}[(X-\mu)^2]$        | Spread around mean |
| Std dev   | $\sigma = \sqrt{\text{Var}(X)}$           | Same units as X    |
| Skewness  | $\mathbb{E}[(X-\mu)^3]/\sigma^3$          | Asymmetry          |
| Kurtosis  | $\mathbb{E}[(X-\mu)^4]/\sigma^4$          | Heavy tails        |

---

## Discrete Distributions

### Bernoulli

Single binary trial. $X \in {0, 1}$

$$ P(X=x) = p^x (1-p)^{1-x} $$

|Stat|Value|
|---|---|
|Mean|$p$|
|Variance|$p(1-p)$|

**In ML:** binary classification output.

---

### Binomial

Number of successes in $n$ independent Bernoulli trials.

$$ P(X=k) = \binom{n}{k} p^k (1-p)^{n-k} $$

|Stat|Value|
|---|---|
|Mean|$np$|
|Variance|$np(1-p)$|

---

### Categorical

Generalization of Bernoulli to $K$ classes.

$$ P(X=k) = p_k, \quad \sum_{k=1}^K p_k = 1 $$

**In ML:** softmax output is a categorical distribution over classes.

---

## Continuous Distributions

### Uniform

$$ f(x) = \frac{1}{b-a}, \quad x \in [a, b] $$

|Stat|Value|
|---|---|
|Mean|$(a+b)/2$|
|Variance|$(b-a)^2/12$|

**In ML:** weight initialization, random sampling.

---

### Gaussian (Normal)

The most important distribution in ML.

$$ f(x) = \frac{1}{\sigma\sqrt{2\pi}} \exp\left(-\frac{(x-\mu)^2}{2\sigma^2}\right) $$

Shorthand: $X \sim \mathcal{N}(\mu, \sigma^2)$

|Stat|Value|
|---|---|
|Mean|$\mu$|
|Variance|$\sigma^2$|

**68-95-99.7 rule:** 68% of values within $1\sigma$, 95% within $2\sigma$, 99.7% within $3\sigma$.

**In ML:** weight init (He, Xavier), noise in diffusion models, VAE latent space.

---

### Multivariate Gaussian

Generalization to vectors $\mathbf{x} \in \mathbb{R}^n$:

$$ f(\mathbf{x}) = \frac{1}{(2\pi)^{n/2}|\Sigma|^{1/2}} \exp\left(-\frac{1}{2}(\mathbf{x}-\boldsymbol{\mu})^\top \Sigma^{-1} (\mathbf{x}-\boldsymbol{\mu})\right) $$

|Term|Meaning|
|---|---|
|$\boldsymbol{\mu} \in \mathbb{R}^n$|Mean vector|
|$\Sigma \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times n}$|Covariance matrix — symmetric, positive definite|

**In ML:** VAE latent prior, Gaussian processes, data modeling.

---

### Covariance Matrix

$$ \Sigma_{ij} = \text{Cov}(X_i, X_j) = \mathbb{E}[(X_i - \mu_i)(X_j - \mu_j)] $$

$$ \Sigma = \begin{bmatrix} \sigma_1^2 & \text{Cov}(X_1,X_2) & \cdots \ \text{Cov}(X_2,X_1) & \sigma_2^2 & \cdots \ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots \end{bmatrix} $$

- Diagonal: variances of each feature
- Off-diagonal: correlations between features
- Always symmetric: $\Sigma = \Sigma^\top$

---

### Exponential

Time between events.

$$ f(x) = \lambda e^{-\lambda x}, \quad x \geq 0 $$

|Stat|Value|
|---|---|
|Mean|$1/\lambda$|
|Variance|$1/\lambda^2$|

---

## Softmax as a Distribution

Softmax converts logits $\mathbf{z}$ into a valid probability distribution:

$$ P(y=k \mid \mathbf{z}) = \frac{e^{z_k}}{\sum_j e^{z_j}} $$

Properties: all values in $(0,1)$, sum to 1 → valid categorical distribution.

## KL Divergence (preview)

Measures how different two distributions are:

$$ D_{KL}(P | Q) = \sum_x P(x) \log \frac{P(x)}{Q(x)} $$

Not symmetric. $D_{KL} = 0$ iff $P = Q$. Full coverage in `Information_Theory/KL_Divergence`.

## Central Limit Theorem

The mean of $n$ i.i.d. samples from **any** distribution converges to a Gaussian as $n \to \infty$:

$$ \bar{X} = \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^n X_i \xrightarrow{d} \mathcal{N}\left(\mu, \frac{\sigma^2}{n}\right) $$

**In ML:** justifies why mini-batch gradient estimates are well-behaved, and why test metrics follow normal distributions.
