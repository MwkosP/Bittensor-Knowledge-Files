


# Information Theory — Mutual Information

---

## Core Idea

Mutual information measures how much knowing one variable reduces uncertainty about another.  
"How much do $X$ and $Y$ share?"

$$ I(X; Y) = H(X) - H(X \mid Y) = H(Y) - H(Y \mid X) $$

---

## Definition

$$ I(X; Y) = \sum_{x,y} P(x,y) \log \frac{P(x,y)}{P(x)P(y)} $$

For continuous distributions:

$$ I(X; Y) = \int\int p(x,y) \log \frac{p(x,y)}{p(x)p(y)} , dx , dy $$

---

## Properties

|Property|Formula / Note|
|---|---|
|Non-negative|$I(X;Y) \geq 0$ always|
|Zero iff independent|$I(X;Y) = 0 \iff X \perp Y$|
|Symmetric|$I(X;Y) = I(Y;X)$|
|Bounded|$I(X;Y) \leq \min(H(X), H(Y))$|

---

## Relation to KL Divergence

Mutual information = KL divergence between joint and product of marginals:

$$ I(X;Y) = D_{KL}(P(X,Y) | P(X)P(Y)) $$

Measures how far the joint distribution is from independence.

---

## Entropy Relationships

$$ \begin{aligned} I(X;Y) &= H(X) + H(Y) - H(X,Y) \\ I(X;Y) &= H(X) - H(X \mid Y) \\ I(X;Y) &= H(Y) - H(Y \mid X) \\ I(X;X) &= H(X) \quad \text{(self-information = entropy)} \end{aligned} $$

---

## Venn Diagram of Entropy

$$ \underbrace{H(X,Y)}_{\text{joint}} = \underbrace{H(X)}_{\text{X only}} + \underbrace{H(Y)}_{\text{Y only}} - \underbrace{I(X;Y)}_{\text{shared}} $$

$$ \begin{aligned} H(X \mid Y) &= H(X) - I(X;Y) \quad \text{(X minus shared)} \ H(Y \mid X) &= H(Y) - I(X;Y) \quad \text{(Y minus shared)} \end{aligned} $$

---

## Example

Two fair dice $X$ and $Y$:

- Independent rolls → $I(X;Y) = 0$
- $Y = X$ (same roll) → $I(X;Y) = H(X) = \log 6 \approx 2.58$ bits
- $Y = X + 1$ (deterministic function) → $I(X;Y) = H(X)$

---

## Conditional Mutual Information

How much does $X$ tell us about $Y$ given we already know $Z$?

$$ I(X;Y \mid Z) = H(X \mid Z) - H(X \mid Y, Z) $$

---

## Normalized Mutual Information (NMI)

Scales MI to $[0, 1]$ for comparisons:

$$ \text{NMI}(X,Y) = \frac{2 \cdot I(X;Y)}{H(X) + H(Y)} $$

**In ML:** evaluation metric for clustering — compare predicted clusters to true labels.

---

## ML Connections

### Feature Selection

High $I(\text{feature}; \text{label})$ → feature is informative → keep it.  
Low $I(\text{feature}; \text{label})$ → feature is irrelevant → drop it.

$$ \text{Select features with highest } I(X_i; Y) $$

### Information Bottleneck

Learn a representation $Z$ of $X$ that:

- Preserves information about $Y$: maximize $I(Z;Y)$
- Compresses $X$: minimize $I(Z;X)$

$$ \min_{p(z|x)} \quad I(Z;X) - \beta \cdot I(Z;Y) $$

Explains why deep networks compress input while retaining task-relevant info.

### Contrastive Learning

Self-supervised methods like SimCLR maximize MI between representations of two augmented views of the same image:

$$ \max_\theta \quad I(Z_1; Z_2) $$

High MI between views = representation captures shared content = good features.

### Mutual Information Neural Estimation (MINE)

MI is hard to compute directly in high dimensions. MINE estimates it via:

$$ I(X;Y) \geq \mathbb{E}_{P(X,Y)}[T_\theta(x,y)] - \log \mathbb{E}_{P(X)P(Y)}[e^{T_\theta(x,y)}] $$

Where $T_\theta$ is a neural network trained to maximize the lower bound.

|ML Concept|Mutual Information Role|

|Information bottleneck|Compress $I(Z;X)$, preserve $I(Z;Y)$|
|Contrastive learning|Maximize $I$ between views|
|Clustering evaluation|NMI between predicted and true clusters|
|Disentanglement|Minimize $I(Z_i; Z_j)$ between latent dims|

## Full Information Theory Map

$$ \begin{aligned} H(X) &= \text{entropy — uncertainty in X} \\ H(X,Y) &= \text{joint entropy — uncertainty in X and Y together} \\ H(X \mid Y) &= \text{conditional entropy — uncertainty in X given Y} \\ H(P,Q) &= \text{cross-entropy — cost of using Q for P} \\ D_{KL}(P | Q) &= \text{KL divergence — extra cost of Q vs P} \\ I(X;Y) &= \text{mutual information — shared information between X and Y} \\ \mathcal{I}(\theta) &= \text{Fisher information — info data carries about } \theta \end{aligned} $$
