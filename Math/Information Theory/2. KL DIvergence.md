




# Information Theory — KL Divergence

---

## Core Idea

KL divergence measures how much one distribution $P$ differs from another $Q$.  
"How much extra information do I need if I use $Q$ instead of the true $P$?"

$$ D_{KL}(P | Q) = \sum_k P(x_k) \log \frac{P(x_k)}{Q(x_k)} $$

For continuous distributions:

$$ D_{KL}(P | Q) = \int p(x) \log \frac{p(x)}{q(x)} dx $$

---

## Properties

|Property|Formula / Note|
|---|---|
|Non-negative|$D_{KL}(P \| Q) \geq 0$ always|
|Zero iff identical|$D_{KL}(P \| Q) = 0 \iff P = Q$|
|Not symmetric|$D_{KL}(P \| Q) \neq D_{KL}(Q \| P)$ in general|
|Not a metric|Triangle inequality doesn't hold|

---

## Relation to Cross-Entropy

$$ D_{KL}(P | Q) = H(P, Q) - H(P) $$

$$ \underbrace{D_{KL}(P | Q)}_{\text{extra cost}} = \underbrace{H(P,Q)}_{\text{cross-entropy}} - \underbrace{H(P)}_{\text{true entropy}} $$

**In ML:** when true labels are one-hot, $H(P) = 0$ — so minimizing cross-entropy = minimizing KL divergence.

---

## Forward vs Reverse KL

|Mode|Formula|Behavior|
|---|---|---|
|Forward KL|$D_{KL}(P \| Q)$|$Q$ must cover all of $P$ — **mean-seeking**|
|Reverse KL|$D_{KL}(Q \| P)$|$Q$ picks one mode of $P$ — **mode-seeking**|

$$ \begin{aligned} \text{Forward KL: } & \text{if } P(x) > 0 \text{ and } Q(x) = 0 \implies D_{KL} = \infty \ \text{Reverse KL: } & Q \text{ avoids regions where } P = 0 \end{aligned} $$

**In ML:**

- Training with cross-entropy = minimizing forward KL
- Variational inference = minimizing reverse KL

---

## KL for Gaussians

Closed form — used constantly in VAEs:

$$ D_{KL}!\left(\mathcal{N}(\mu, \sigma^2) | \mathcal{N}(0, 1)\right) = \frac{1}{2}\left(\mu^2 + \sigma^2 - \ln\sigma^2 - 1\right) $$

For multivariate:

$$ D_{KL}!\left(\mathcal{N}(\boldsymbol{\mu}, \boldsymbol{\Sigma}) | \mathcal{N}(0, I)\right) = \frac{1}{2}\left(\text{tr}(\boldsymbol{\Sigma}) + \boldsymbol{\mu}^\top\boldsymbol{\mu} - d - \ln\det(\boldsymbol{\Sigma})\right) $$

---

## VAE Loss (ELBO)

The VAE loss combines reconstruction and KL:

$$ \mathcal{L} = \underbrace{\mathbb{E}_{q(z|x)}[\log p(x|z)]}_{\text{reconstruction loss}} - \underbrace{D_{KL}(q(z|x) | p(z))}_{\text{regularization}} $$

|Term|Meaning|
|---|---|
|Reconstruction|How well decoder reconstructs input|
|KL term|Forces latent space toward prior $\mathcal{N}(0,I)$|
|$\beta$-VAE|Multiply KL by $\beta > 1$ for disentanglement|

---

## PPO Clipping and KL

PPO (Proximal Policy Optimization) uses KL to limit policy updates:

$$ D_{KL}(\pi_{\text{old}} | \pi_{\text{new}}) \leq \delta $$

Too large a KL = policy changed too much = training instability.  
PPO clips the importance ratio instead of directly constraining KL — faster to compute.

---

## Knowledge Distillation

Student learns from teacher's soft output distribution:

$$ L = \alpha \cdot H(y, \hat{y}_s) + (1-\alpha) \cdot D_{KL}(\hat{y}_t^T | \hat{y}_s^T) $$

|Term|Meaning|
|---|---|
|$\hat{y}_t^T$|Teacher soft labels at temperature $T$|
|$\hat{y}_s^T$|Student soft labels at temperature $T$|
|$\alpha$|Balance between hard and soft targets|

Teacher's soft labels have higher entropy → richer signal than one-hot.

---

## Jensen-Shannon Divergence

Symmetric version of KL — used in GANs:

$$ D_{JS}(P | Q) = \frac{1}{2} D_{KL}(P | M) + \frac{1}{2} D_{KL}(Q | M), \quad M = \frac{P+Q}{2} $$

|Property|Value|
|---|---|
|Range|$[0, \log 2]$|
|Symmetric|Yes|
|Zero iff identical|Yes|

Original GAN training = minimizing JS divergence between real and generated distributions.
