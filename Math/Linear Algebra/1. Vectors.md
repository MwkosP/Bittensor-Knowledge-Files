



# Linear Algebra — Vectors

---

## What a Vector Is

An ordered list of numbers. Represents a point or direction in space.

$$ \mathbf{v} = \begin{bmatrix} v_1 \ v_2 \ \vdots \ v_n \end{bmatrix} \in \mathbb{R}^n $$

In ML: a single data point, a word embedding, a model's hidden state — all vectors.

---

## Notation

|Symbol|Meaning|
|---|---|
|`v ∈ ℝⁿ`|Vector of n real numbers|
|`vᵀ`|Transpose (row vector)|
|`vᵢ`|i-th element of v|
|`‖v‖`|Norm (length) of v|

---

## Basic Operations

|Operation|Formula|Note|
|---|---|---|
|Addition|`u + v = [u₁+v₁, ..., uₙ+vₙ]`|Element-wise|
|Scalar mult|`αv = [αv₁, ..., αvₙ]`|Scales the vector|
|Subtraction|`u - v = u + (-1)v`|Direction between two points|

---

## Dot Product

$$ \mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{v} = \mathbf{u}^\top \mathbf{v} = \sum_i u_i v_i $$

|Property|Formula|
|---|---|
|Commutative|`u · v = v · u`|
|Geometric form|`u · v = ‖u‖ ‖v‖ cos(θ)`|
|Self dot product|`v · v = ‖v‖²`|

**In ML:** attention scores, similarity between embeddings, neuron activations — all dot products.

---

## Norms (Vector Length)

|Norm|Formula|Use|
|---|---|---|
|L1|`‖v‖₁ = Σ\|vᵢ\|`|Sparse regularization (Lasso)|
|L2 (Euclidean)|`‖v‖₂ = √(Σvᵢ²)`|Default distance, weight decay|
|L∞|`‖v‖∞ = max\|vᵢ\|`|Worst-case bound|

---

## Unit Vectors & Normalization

$$ \hat{\mathbf{v}} = \frac{\mathbf{v}}{|\mathbf{v}|} $$

**In ML:** normalizing embeddings before dot product → cosine similarity.

---

## Cosine Similarity

$$ \cos(\theta) = \frac{\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{v}}{|\mathbf{u}| |\mathbf{v}|} \in [-1, 1] $$

|Value|Meaning|
|---|---|
|1|Same direction (identical)|
|0|Orthogonal (unrelated)|
|-1|Opposite direction|

**In ML:** semantic similarity between word/sentence embeddings.

---

## Orthogonality

Two vectors are orthogonal if:

$$ \mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{v} = 0 $$

Orthogonal vectors carry no shared information — useful in PCA and SVD.

---

## Projection

Project vector **u** onto vector **v**:

$$ \text{proj}_\mathbf{v}(\mathbf{u}) = \frac{\mathbf{u} \cdot \mathbf{v}}{|\mathbf{v}|^2} \cdot \mathbf{v} $$

**In ML:** attention is a soft projection — each query projects onto keys to retrieve values.
