



# Calculus — Multivariable (Partial Derivatives, Jacobian, Hessian)

---

## Partial Derivatives (recap)

Derivative of a function with respect to **one variable**, holding all others fixed.

$$ f(x, y) = x^2 y + 3y^2 $$

$$ \begin{aligned} \frac{\partial f}{\partial x} &= 2xy \ \frac{\partial f}{\partial y} &= x^2 + 6y \end{aligned} $$

Second-order partial — derivative of a derivative:

$$ \frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x^2} = 2y \qquad \frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x \partial y} = 2x $$

---

## Jacobian

When a function maps **vectors to vectors** $f: \mathbb{R}^n \to \mathbb{R}^m$, the Jacobian is the matrix of all first-order partial derivatives.

$$ J = \frac{\partial \mathbf{f}}{\partial \mathbf{x}} = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial f_1}{\partial x_1} & \frac{\partial f_1}{\partial x_2} & \cdots & \frac{\partial f_1}{\partial x_n} \\[6pt] \frac{\partial f_2}{\partial x_1} & \frac{\partial f_2}{\partial x_2} & \cdots & \frac{\partial f_2}{\partial x_n} \\[6pt] \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\[6pt] \frac{\partial f_m}{\partial x_1} & \frac{\partial f_m}{\partial x_2} & \cdots & \frac{\partial f_m}{\partial x_n} \end{bmatrix} \in \mathbb{R}^{m \times n} $$

|Shape|Meaning|
|---|---|
|`m = 1`|Gradient (row vector)|
|`m = n`|Square Jacobian|
|General|Linear approximation of f near a point|

### Example — Softmax Jacobian

Softmax $\sigma: \mathbb{R}^n \to \mathbb{R}^n$:

$$ \frac{\partial \sigma_i}{\partial z_j} = \begin{cases} \sigma_i(1 - \sigma_i) & \text{if } i = j \ -\sigma_i \sigma_j & \text{if } i \neq j \end{cases} $$

**In ML:** during backprop, every layer transformation $f: \mathbb{R}^n \to \mathbb{R}^m$ has a Jacobian. The chain rule in vector form is:

$$ \frac{\partial L}{\partial \mathbf{x}} = J^\top \frac{\partial L}{\partial \mathbf{f}} $$

## Hessian

When $f: \mathbb{R}^n \to \mathbb{R}$ (scalar output), the Hessian is the matrix of all **second-order** partial derivatives.

$$ H = \nabla^2 f = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x_1^2} & \frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x_1 \partial x_2} & \cdots & \frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x_1 \partial x_n} \\[6pt] \frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x_2 \partial x_1} & \frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x_2^2} & \cdots & \frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x_2 \partial x_n} \\[6pt] \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\[6pt] \frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x_n \partial x_1} & \frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x_n \partial x_2} & \cdots & \frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x_n^2} \end{bmatrix} \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times n} $$

- Always **symmetric**: $H_{ij} = H_{ji}$
- Captures **curvature** — how the gradient itself is changing

---

## What the Hessian Tells You

The eigenvalues of H reveal the shape of the loss surface at a point:

|Hessian eigenvalues|Point type|Meaning|
|---|---|---|
|All $\lambda > 0$|Local minimum|Loss curves up in all directions|
|All $\lambda < 0$|Local maximum|Loss curves down in all directions|
|Mixed signs|Saddle point|Curves up in some, down in others|
|Some $\lambda = 0$|Flat region|No curvature — plateau|

### Condition Number

$$ \kappa = \frac{\lambda_\text{max}}{\lambda_\text{min}} $$

High condition number → loss surface is like a long narrow valley → gradient descent oscillates, slow to converge.

---

## Second-Order Taylor Expansion

The Hessian appears in the local quadratic approximation of the loss:

$$ L(\mathbf{w} + \delta) \approx L(\mathbf{w}) + \nabla L^\top \delta + \frac{1}{2} \delta^\top H \delta $$

This is the foundation of **second-order optimizers** (Newton's method, natural gradient).

---

## Newton's Method

Instead of stepping opposite to gradient, step using curvature info:

$$ \mathbf{w} \leftarrow \mathbf{w} - H^{-1} \nabla L $$

| | Gradient Descent | Newton's Method |
|---|---|---|
| Update | $-\eta \nabla L$ | $-H^{-1} \nabla L$ |
| Info used | First-order (slope) | Second-order (curvature) |
| Cost per step | Cheap | $O(n^3)$ — inverting H |
| Use in ML | Always | Rarely — too expensive |

**In practice:** Adam approximates curvature cheaply using running variance of gradients.

---

## Gradient vs Jacobian vs Hessian

|Object|Input → Output|Contains|
|---|---|---|
|Gradient $\nabla f$|$\mathbb{R}^n \to \mathbb{R}$|First derivatives — vector|
|Jacobian $J$|$\mathbb{R}^n \to \mathbb{R}^m$|First derivatives — matrix|
|Hessian $H$|$\mathbb{R}^n \to \mathbb{R}$|Second derivatives — matrix|
