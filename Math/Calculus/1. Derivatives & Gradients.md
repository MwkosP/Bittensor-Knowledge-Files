


# Calculus — Derivatives & Gradients

---

## Derivative (1D)

Rate of change of a function at a point. How much does output change when input changes slightly?(RATE OF CHANGE)

$$ f'(x) = \frac{df}{dx} = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h} $$

Geometrically: **slope of the tangent line** at x.

|Function|Derivative|

## Derivative Rules

|Rule|Formula|
|---|---|
|Sum|$(f + g)' = f' + g'$|
|Product|$(fg)' = f'g + fg'$|
|Quotient|$(f/g)' = (f'g - fg') / g^2$|
|Chain rule|$(f(g(x)))' = f'(g(x)) \cdot g'(x)$|
|Constant|$(cf)' = cf'$|

## Partial Derivatives

For functions of multiple variables — derivative with respect to **one variable**, holding others fixed.

$$ f(x, y) = x^2 + 3xy + y^2 $$

$$ \begin{aligned} \frac{\partial f}{\partial x} &= 2x + 3y \ \frac{\partial f}{\partial y} &= 3x + 2y \end{aligned} $$

**In [[ML]]:** loss `L` is a function of millions of weights. Each $\partial L / \partial w_i$ tells you how `L` changes w.r.t. one weight.(the RATE of change)

---

## The Gradient

Collect all partial derivatives into a vector — that's the **gradient**.

$$ \nabla_\mathbf{w} L = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial L}{\partial w_1} \ \frac{\partial L}{\partial w_2} \ \vdots \ \frac{\partial L}{\partial w_n} \end{bmatrix} $$

|Property|Meaning|
|---|---|
|Direction|Points toward steepest **increase** of L|
|Magnitude|How steep the slope is|
|Zero gradient|At a minimum, maximum, or saddle point|

To **minimize** loss: move opposite to the gradient.

---

## Gradient Descent

$$ \mathbf{w} \leftarrow \mathbf{w} - \eta \nabla_\mathbf{w} L $$

|Symbol|Meaning|
|---|---|
|$\mathbf{w}$|Weight vector|
|$\eta$|Learning rate|
|$\nabla_\mathbf{w} L$|Gradient of loss w.r.t. weights|

Each step moves weights in the direction that **reduces** the loss.

---

## Directional Derivative

How fast does `f` change in a specific direction `u`?

$$ D_\mathbf{u} f = \nabla f \cdot \mathbf{u} = |\nabla f| \cos(\theta) $$

Maximum increase: direction of $\nabla f$.  
Maximum decrease: direction of $-\nabla f$ ← **this is what gradient descent uses**.

---

## Gradient of Common ML Functions

|Function|Gradient|
|---|---|
|$L = \frac{1}{2}\|\mathbf{w}\|^2$|$\nabla L = \mathbf{w}$|
|$L = \mathbf{w}^\top \mathbf{x}$|$\nabla_\mathbf{w} L = \mathbf{x}$|
|$L = \frac{1}{2}(y - \mathbf{w}^\top \mathbf{x})^2$|$\nabla_\mathbf{w} L = -(y - \mathbf{w}^\top \mathbf{x})\mathbf{x}$|
|$L = -\log \sigma(\mathbf{w}^\top \mathbf{x})$|$\nabla_\mathbf{w} L = (\sigma(\mathbf{w}^\top \mathbf{x}) - 1)\mathbf{x}$|

## Numerical vs Analytical Gradient

|Type|How|Use|
|---|---|---|
|Analytical|Derive with calculus rules|Training — exact and fast|
|Numerical|$\frac{f(x+\epsilon) - f(x-\epsilon)}{2\epsilon}$|Gradient checking / debugging|

**Gradient checking:** verify backprop is correct by comparing analytical gradient to numerical estimate.

## ML Connections

|ML Concept|Derivative Concept|
|---|---|
|Training = minimizing loss|Gradient descent on $\nabla L$|
|Backpropagation|Chain rule applied layer by layer|
|Weight update|$w \leftarrow w - \eta \partial L / \partial w$|
|Vanishing gradient|$\partial L / \partial w \approx 0$ in early layers|
|Exploding gradient|$\partial L / \partial w \to \infty$|
|Adam, RMSProp|Adaptive scaling of gradients|
